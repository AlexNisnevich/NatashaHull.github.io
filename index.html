Bio

After dabbling in politics and acting, I went to UC Berkeley and studied Music and Philosophy. I discovered that I was particularly passionate about logic, but spent a lot of my time as an undergrad figuring out what I would do after I graduated.

Early in my last semester at UC Berkeley, I went to a RailsBridge (a programming event for women that uses scaffolding to demonstrate Ruby on Rails). From there, I started creating and experimenting with Ruby on Rails applications (the main one being Cocktail Enthusiasts), as my first excursion into programming. I had previously worked with HTML before, but had not yet tried programming. I quickly realized that all the things I loved about logic were also important for programming and have continued programming since.

I am currently looking for a position as a backend web developer. At the moment, I have worked with Ruby, Rails, SQL, JavaScript, jQuery, and Backbone. (For one project, I built a full stack Rails application and then added Backbone).
